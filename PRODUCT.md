# Product

## Register

product

## Users

PPT God serves people who need to turn real materials, rough ideas, and follow-up edits into usable presentation decks. They are often working with source documents, page-count expectations, visual direction, and delivery pressure at the same time. The interface should help them keep intent, materials, deck state, and next action visible without exposing internal implementation terms.

## Product Purpose

PPT God is an Agent-driven presentation tool. Its purpose is to understand the user's intent, produce a presentation artifact that respects that intent, and keep the user oriented while the deck is planned, refined, generated, reviewed, and revised.

Success means the user can see what the system understands, what it is doing now, what needs their attention, and where to change course before quality is lost.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, and editorial. PPT God should feel like a serious content director with strong visual taste: confident about structure, transparent about state, and careful with source fidelity.

## Anti-references

- Generic slide-template galleries that make the user assemble everything manually.
- Chat-only AI tools that promise changes without applying them to the deck.
- Overloaded AI dashboards that expose model routing, internal modes, or storage details.
- Decorative SaaS surfaces that hide the current artifact, source coverage, or next action.

## Design Principles

- Keep the artifact at the center: every major screen should make the current deck or the next deck decision visible.
- Translate technical distinctions into user outcomes before showing them in the UI.
- Match Agent language to executable product state; never claim completion before state changes.
- Prefer clear preflight, review, and retry states over silent fallback.
- Make project boundaries visible enough that users trust they are editing the right deck.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The product UI should meet WCAG AA contrast for text and controls, support keyboard navigation across core actions, preserve meaning without color alone, and provide reduced-motion alternatives for transitions.
